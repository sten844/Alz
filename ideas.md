# Design Brainstorm: Jag och min Alzheimer

## Context
This is a personal knowledge base / blog about living with Alzheimer's disease. The author (Sten Dellby) shares articles about treatment plans, research, daily life, and medications. The site must feel warm, trustworthy, and accessible — not clinical or cold. It needs bilingual support (Swedish/English) and clear navigation.

## Idea 1: "Nordic Warmth" — Scandinavian Editorial Design

**Design Movement**: Scandinavian editorial design meets healthcare accessibility. Inspired by Swedish design magazines like Kinfolk and Scandinavian healthcare communication.

**Core Principles**:
1. Generous whitespace as a calming, cognitive-friendly element
2. Warm neutral tones that feel like a Swedish home, not a hospital
3. Clear typographic hierarchy for easy scanning and reading
4. Content-first layout that respects the reader's cognitive load

**Color Philosophy**: A palette rooted in Swedish nature — soft sky blues (representing clarity and hope), warm sand/wheat tones (grounding and comfort), with muted coral accents (warmth without alarm). The blue gradient background from the original site is preserved but refined.

**Layout Paradigm**: Asymmetric editorial grid with generous margins. Articles use a magazine-style layout with pull quotes and clear section breaks. The homepage uses a stacked editorial approach rather than a rigid grid.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Soft watercolor-like gradient backgrounds transitioning between sections
2. Rounded card elements with subtle paper-like texture
3. Warm photographic treatment with soft overlays

**Interaction Philosophy**: Gentle, predictable interactions. No surprises. Smooth scrolling, subtle hover states that enlarge slightly. Everything feels calm and intentional.

**Animation**: Fade-in on scroll with gentle vertical translation (8-12px). Cards lift slightly on hover with soft shadow expansion. Page transitions use a simple crossfade. Nothing jarring.

**Typography System**: 
- Display: DM Serif Display — warm, readable serif for headlines
- Body: Source Sans 3 — highly legible, friendly sans-serif
- Hierarchy: Large display titles (3rem), clear subheads (1.5rem), comfortable body (1.125rem with 1.75 line-height)

## Idea 2: "Gentle Brutalism" — Structured Clarity

**Design Movement**: Soft brutalism meets medical information design. Bold structure with warm execution.

**Core Principles**:
1. Bold structural elements that create unmistakable navigation
2. High contrast for accessibility without harshness
3. Chunky, confident typography that's easy to read
4. Color-coded categories as a wayfinding system

**Color Philosophy**: Deep charcoal backgrounds for headers with warm cream content areas. Each category gets a distinct but harmonious color: teal for Behandling, amber for Forskning, sage green for Vardagsliv, dusty rose for Läkemedel, slate blue for Åsikt.

**Layout Paradigm**: Stacked blocks with bold dividers. Each section is a distinct "card" with thick borders. Navigation uses a prominent sidebar on desktop, bottom tabs on mobile.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Thick 3px borders on cards and sections
2. Category color bars on article cards (left border accent)
3. Monospace date stamps contrasting with serif headlines

**Interaction Philosophy**: Direct and immediate. Hover states use bold color fills. Buttons have visible press states. Everything communicates "you clicked this, here's what happened."

**Animation**: Minimal — only essential transitions. Cards snap into view. No floating or drifting. Quick 150ms transitions.

**Typography System**:
- Display: Space Grotesk — geometric, modern, highly readable
- Body: IBM Plex Sans — clinical precision with warmth
- Monospace accents: IBM Plex Mono for dates and metadata

## Idea 3: "Living Document" — Organic Knowledge Garden

**Design Movement**: Digital garden / living notebook aesthetic. Inspired by personal wikis and hand-annotated medical journals.

**Core Principles**:
1. Content feels like a growing, living collection — not a static archive
2. Connections between articles are visible and explorable
3. Personal voice is embedded in the design itself
4. Reading experience prioritizes long-form comfort

**Color Philosophy**: Warm parchment base (#faf8f5) with ink-dark text (#2d2a26). Accent colors drawn from nature: forest green for growth/treatment, ocean blue for research depth, sunset orange for daily life warmth, medicinal purple for pharmaceuticals.

**Layout Paradigm**: Single-column reading layout with a floating side navigation. Articles flow like chapters in a book. The homepage is a curated "table of contents" with the most recent additions highlighted.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Subtle paper texture background
2. Handwritten-style category labels (using a script font sparingly)
3. Connected article suggestions shown as "related threads"

**Interaction Philosophy**: Exploratory and gentle. Hover reveals connections. Clicking feels like turning a page. The site invites browsing rather than searching.

**Animation**: Page-turn-like transitions. Content fades in as if being written. Scroll-linked subtle parallax on hero sections.

**Typography System**:
- Display: Playfair Display — elegant, bookish serif
- Body: Lora — comfortable reading serif
- Accents: Caveat for handwritten touches on labels

## Selected Approach: Idea 1 — "Nordic Warmth"

This approach best serves the target audience (people with Alzheimer's and their caregivers) because:
- The generous whitespace and clear hierarchy reduce cognitive load
- The warm, home-like color palette creates trust and comfort
- The editorial layout makes medical information feel approachable
- The typography system balances warmth (serif headlines) with readability (sans-serif body)
- It closely matches the existing site's visual language while elevating the design quality
